CLINICAL PROTOCOL: HYPERTENSION MANAGEMENT
Date: 2026-05-31
Department: Cardiology

For patients presenting with Stage 2 Hypertension (blood pressure > 140/90 mmHg), the first line of pharmacological treatment should include an ACE inhibitor (e.g., Lisinopril) or an Angiotensin II Receptor Blocker (ARB) such as Losartan.

If the patient is over 60 years of age, calcium channel blockers (e.g., Amlodipine) are preferred due to increased efficacy in older populations. Patient follow-up should be scheduled exactly 14 days after initial medication administration to monitor renal function and potassium levels.
